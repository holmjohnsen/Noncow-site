Why Almond & Coconut Milk Fall Short

If protein is your priority, skip these:

Almond Milk: Only 1g protein per cup. It's low-calorie and great for weight loss, but terrible for protein.

Coconut Milk: 0-1g protein. High in fat, low in everything else. Good for baking, not for nutrition.

Cashew Milk: 1g protein. Creamy and delicious, but not a protein source.

Best Ways to Use High-Protein Plant Milk

1. Protein Smoothies

The #1 use case. Blend soy or pea milk with bananas, berries, spinach, protein powder, nut butter, and seeds. You'll easily hit 20-30g of protein per smoothie.

2. Post-Workout Recovery

Drink a glass of soy or pea milk within 30 minutes after exercise. The protein helps repair muscle tissue and supports recovery.

3. Breakfast Boost

Use high-protein milk on cereal or oatmeal. Add some nuts or seeds for even more protein.

4. Coffee & Lattes

Both soy and protein-fortified oat milk work great in coffee and froth well for lattes. Start your day with a protein boost!

‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

What plant milk has the most protein?

Soy milk and pea milk tie for the highest protein content at 7-9g and 8g per cup respectively. Both contain complete proteins with all 9 essential amino acids, making them comparable to cow's milk which has 8g per cup.

Does oat milk have protein?

Yes, but not much. Regular oat milk contains only 2-4g of protein per cup, about half of what soy or pea milk provides. Some brands now fortify oat milk with pea protein to boost the protein content to 8g per cup.

Is plant milk protein complete?

Soy milk and pea milk contain complete proteins with all 9 essential amino acids your body needs. Oat, almond, coconut, and cashew milk have incomplete proteins that lack one or more essential amino acids.

Which plant milk is best for building muscle?

Soy milk is best for building muscle because it provides 7-9g of complete protein per cup at an affordable price. Pea milk is a close second with 8g of complete protein per cup. Both support muscle recovery and growth when consumed after workouts.

How much protein is in almond milk?

Almond milk contains only 1g of protein per cup, making it one of the lowest-protein plant milks. While great for low-calorie diets, almond milk is not a good protein source. Choose soy or pea milk if protein is your priority.

Can I use plant milk in protein shakes?

Yes! Soy milk and pea milk are excellent bases for protein shakes. They add 7-9g of complete protein before you even add protein powder, helping you easily reach 25-35g of protein per shake. They also blend smoothly and taste neutral.
